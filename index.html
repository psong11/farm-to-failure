Where does obesity persist in the world?


Use the slider under the french fry visualization to see how obesity rates have changed over time.

By using the slide you will see that the Oceanian country Nauru maintains the highest obesity rate in the world from 1975 to 2016. Most of the other top 10 countries are also in Oceania, which highlights the importance of geography in the obesity epidemic.
According to the World Health Organization, the advent of fast food and the rise of sedentary lifestyles are the main causes of the obesity epidemic.

    Resolution

    In the end, we just want to reiterate our message that fast food has negative effects on society, albeit very tasty to eat once in a while. It is important that society is aware of the effects that it has and the correlations it implies with other health factors like mental health. Long story short, if we care about our future generations, we need to change the way we look at fast food.
